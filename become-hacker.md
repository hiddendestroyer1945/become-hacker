# Become a Hacker
* **Description**:  To explain about the, how to become a hacker, who are the hacker, types of hackers, roles and responsibilities of a hackers and steps to become a hacker.

* **Become a Hacker**: Hacking is not a career-oriented field. All those who have achieved success in the field of hacking have developed their own knowledge and training in hacking out of a passion for it.

* **Hacker**: A hacker is a person who uses their technical skills to break into computer systems and networks, often for malicious purposes. Hackers can be classified into three types: white hat, black hat, and grey hat. But becoming a hacker requires a combination of technical knowledge and a creative problem-solving mindset.

* **Types of Hackers**: There are three main types of hackers in the cybersecurity field: black hat, white hat, and gray hat. These are explained below.

  * **Black Hat Hacker**: A black hat hacker is a malicious hacker who uses their skills to break into computer systems and networks for the purpose of stealing data and causing damage. Black hat hackers are often associated with cybercrime and are responsible for many of the most well-known cyber attacks.
  * **White Hat Hacker**: A white hat hacker is an ethical hacker who uses their skills to break into computer systems and networks for the purpose of finding vulnerabilities and fixing them. White hat hackers are often associated with cybersecurity.
  * **Gray Hat Hacker**: A gray hat hacker is a hacker who uses their skills to break into computer systems and networks for the purpose of both ethical and malicious purposes. Gray hat hackers are often associated with both cybersecurity and cybercrime.

* **Roles and Responsibilities of a Hackers**: The roles and responsibilities of a hacker are as follows.

  * **Closing the Weak Ports**: Hackers prevent cyber attacks by finding weak ports on computers and networks and closing them.
  * **Patch Testing**: Hackers check patch installation to ensure they do not lead to cyberattacks.
  * **Preventing Information Leakage**: Hackers prevent this information from being leaked by ensuring that sensitive data is not subject to unauthorized access and that sensitive data is not available on the internet or social media platforms.
  * **Defensive System Development**: Hackers are developing defensive measures to increase the security of networks and reduce the risk of cyberattacks.
  * **Security Breach Reporting**: Hackers if find the security breaches that report  to their institutions to prevent further damage.
  * **Prevent the Vulnerability**: Security vulnerabilities discovered during security audits are fixed to prevent hackers from exploiting them.
